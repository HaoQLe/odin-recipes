Pho

Original Recipe Source

Description

Pho is a traditional Vietnamese soup that combines clear beef broth, rice noodles known as "banh pho," herbs, and meat, typically beef (pho bo) or chicken (pho ga). Its origins trace back to the early 20th century in Northern Vietnam, and it has since become a staple dish in Vietnamese cuisine, popular worldwide. The broth is simmered for several hours with spices such as cinnamon, star anise, cloves, and cardamom, contributing to its distinctive, aromatic flavor. Served with a variety of fresh garnishes like basil, lime, bean sprouts, and sliced chili, pho is celebrated for its complex flavors and textures, offering a comforting and customizable eating experience.

Ingredients

  • Beef Soup Bones: Use the shank and knee to make the homemade bone broth.
  • Rice Noodles: You should be able to find dry rice noodles at most grocery stores, either in the international aisle or another pantry aisle.
  • Beef: This recipe calls for beef top sirloin. Make sure it's thinly sliced so the hot broth can cook it.
  • Fish Sauce: Fish sauce will give the pho a hint of salty, fishy flavor.
  • Onion: Roast the onions for an extra depth of flavor.
  • Spices and Seasonings: This recipe calls for ginger, star anise, and salt.

Steps

  1. Roast the beef bones and onions until the bones are browned and the onions are soft.
  2. Make the broth.
  3. Soak the rice noodles for an hour. Meanwhile, bring the broth to a simmer.
  4. Boil the noodles, then drain and add them to a bowl.
  5. Top with sirloin, cilantro, green onion, and broth. Garnish with your desired toppings.
